# 🔥 FIRE: Hackathon Demo Script (3 Minutes)
*This script is designed for a fast-paced, high-impact video edit. The actual chat prompts are de-emphasized. Instead, the video will use quick interstitial title cards (fades) to separate the phases, while the UI results play in the background and the voiceover focuses on the grand ideas and business impact.*

---

### [0:00 - 0:30] Introduction: The Market Failure
**Visual:** Quick fade in. Title Card: **"FIRE: FHIR-Integrated Revenue Engine"**. Fade to the Prompt Opinion platform UI.
**Audio:**
"Hospitals are currently bleeding millions of dollars in Medicare revenue simply because their clinical documentation isn't specific enough to meet the new, incredibly strict CMS V28 requirements. 

Enter FIRE: The FHIR-Integrated Revenue Engine. 

FIRE is a deterministic, multi-agent AI pipeline that directly interfaces with live FHIR R4 servers to automatically audit patient records for undocumented coding gaps. Built on the Prompt Opinion platform, we use the SHARP protocol to ensure we are fully compliant with Darena Health and HTI-1 mandates. This means FIRE plugs into any compliant EHR instantly."

---

### [0:30 - 1:00] Step 1: Cohort Triage
**Visual:** Interstitial Fade. Title Card: **"Step 1: Cohort Triage & Baseline"**. Fade to the Orchestrator returning the markdown scorecard in the background.
**Audio:**
"Let's see it in action. Our first step isn't just a data pull; it's financial triage.

Our Clinical Orchestrator reaches out to a live, public HAPI FHIR server and generates our Baseline Scorecard. All we are doing here is establishing the current financial baseline for the hospital—calculating exactly what each patient's currently coded conditions are worth. 

More importantly, the system scans the FHIR records to flag exactly who has unreviewed, unstructured clinical notes. We aren't guessing; we are prioritizing the exact patients hiding lost revenue."

---

### [1:00 - 1:45] Step 2: Deterministic Risk Analysis 
**Visual:** Interstitial Fade. Title Card: **"Step 2: Deterministic Risk Analysis"**. Fade to the Risk Navigator outputting the identified V28 gaps and RAF math.
**Audio:**
"Next, we deploy the HCC Risk Navigator. This is where our multi-agent architecture shines. 

Most AI healthcare tools fail because they let the LLM summarize data, leading to hallucinations and context fragmentation. We don't do that. Our Orchestrator passes the *entire raw JSON array* in a single, deterministic hand-off to the Navigator. 

The Navigator acts as our specialized analyst. It cross-references the raw clinical notes against the CMS V28 dictionary. Instantly, it finds hidden, high-value gaps for Tamara, Richard, and Maria—calculating the exact projected RAF increases and proving the precise financial impact for each."

---

### [1:45 - 2:15] Step 3: The Zero-Trust Firewall
**Visual:** Interstitial Fade. Title Card: **"Step 3: The Zero-Trust Compliance Firewall"**. Fade to the Compliance Reviewer showing green checkmarks.
**Audio:**
"But finding a gap isn't enough; you have to legally prove it. We pass these findings to our third agent: The Compliance Reviewer. 

This agent is our zero-trust firewall. It has absolutely no direct access to the FHIR database. Its sole purpose is to act as an independent auditor to prevent fraud. It rigorously verifies that every single proposed ICD-10 code is backed by strict CMS M.E.A.T. criteria—meaning the doctor explicitly Monitored, Evaluated, Assessed, or Treated the condition in the text. 

If it's not in the note, it doesn't pass."

---

### [2:15 - 3:00] Step 4: The 5Ts & Business Model
**Visual:** Interstitial Fade. Title Card: **"Step 4: The Final Deliverable"**. Fade to the final 5Ts report generating, showing the Physician Query drafts.
**Audio:**
"Finally, the pipeline compiles a complete deliverable, including fully customized Physician Query drafts ready to be signed. In this single sweep, we've recovered $9,540 in immediate revenue across just three patients.

Our business model is pure shared-savings: Minimal upfront SaaS fees, taking exactly 10% of the net new revenue generated. 

With a conservative estimate of finding just a 5% gap prevalence, FIRE generates $100,000 in Annual Recurring Revenue per mid-sized hospital. Capturing just 10 hospitals makes FIRE a $1 Million ARR business with near-zero marginal costs. 

That is the power of the FHIR-Integrated Revenue Engine."
